Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about early cashout and hedge betting

What is an early cashout in sports betting?

Early cashout allows you to settle your bet before the event finishes. The cashout amount is calculated based on the current odds compared to your original odds. By hedging — placing a second bet on the opposite outcome — you can lock in a guaranteed profit or minimize potential losses regardless of the final result.

How is the hedge amount calculated?

The hedge amount is calculated using this formula:

Hedge Amount = (Original Stake × Original Odds) / New Odds

This gives you the exact amount to bet at the current odds to match the payout of your original bet. By adjusting this amount slightly, you can distribute profit across both outcomes.

When should I cash out early on a sports bet?

Consider cashing out when:

  • The odds have moved significantly in your favor and you want to lock in a guaranteed profit
  • You want to reduce risk exposure on a large bet
  • Circumstances of the event have changed (injuries, red cards, weather conditions)
  • You have insider knowledge that the situation might change
  • You've hit your profit target for the day/week

What is the difference between cashout and hedging?

Cashout is a feature offered by bookmakers that lets you settle a bet early for a fixed amount. The bookmaker calculates this and takes a margin.

Hedging is when you manually place a second bet on the opposite outcome at the current odds. This often gives better value than the bookmaker's cashout offer because you avoid their margin.
